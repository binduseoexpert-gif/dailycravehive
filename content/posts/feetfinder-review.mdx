## What Is FeetFinder?

FeetFinder is a dedicated online marketplace for buying and selling foot-related photos, videos, and custom content. It's operated by FLRT Inc., and unlike selling through Instagram DMs or Reddit, everything happens inside one structured platform: seller profiles, content listings, buyer messaging, custom requests, and payments.

The single biggest difference from social media selling is **verification**. Every seller must be 18 or older and verify their identity with a government-issued photo ID before accessing seller features. What FeetFinder is *not*: a passive income button. It's a marketplace — and like any marketplace, results depend on your profile quality, pricing, consistency, and how professionally you run your page.

## Is FeetFinder Safe?

The platform side is reasonably solid: identity verification, on-platform payments, content rules, and no requirement to show your face. The bigger safety variable is **you**. Most privacy disasters come from seller mistakes, not platform failures.

Non-negotiable safety habits for sellers:

- **Use a completely separate identity.** New username, not connected to any personal social account or email you've used elsewhere.
- **Never share personal details** — full name, phone, address, workplace, or personal socials, no matter how friendly a buyer seems.
- **Check every photo background.** Mail, documents, family photos, street views, mirrors, distinctive decor, car plates — all of it can identify you. Zoom in before uploading.
- **Strip photo metadata** before uploading, since image files can contain location data.
- **Keep all chat and payment on-platform.** The moment someone pushes you to WhatsApp, Telegram, or a payment app, treat it as a scam signal.
- **Never send free "samples" or unpaid customs.** This is the most common beginner trap — it produces free-content hunters, not sales.
- **State your boundaries in your bio** and never accept requests outside them or outside platform rules.

Buyers have a shorter list: read seller descriptions, respect stated boundaries, never request prohibited content, and keep payments on the platform.

## Beginner Tips That Actually Move the Needle

**Profile:** Write a short, confident bio — what you offer, how customs work, your boundaries, your response time. "Welcome — I create clean, creative foot content with custom requests available within my listed boundaries" reads professional. "Need money fast, cheap pics, DM me" reads like a scam.

**Content quality:** You don't need a studio. Natural window light, a clean background, neat framing, tidy nails, and a consistent style beat expensive gear. Organize around simple categories — barefoot, socks, heels, nail polish, seasonal themes — instead of random uploads.

**Pricing:** Too low signals low value and attracts bad buyers; too high stalls a profile with zero reviews. Start fair, watch what sells, adjust. Customs should always cost more than ready-made content — price the effort, and never promise unlimited revisions.

**Previews:** Tease, don't give away. Crop or watermark preview images so they can't be reused for free.
